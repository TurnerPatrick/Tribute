Derrick Rose

Rose From the Concrete

Things to know about Derrick Rose
  • Born in Chicago on Oct. 24, 1988 and was raised in the Englewood community.
  • Derrick Rose is the youngest MVP in league history. At age 22 he beat out stars such as Kobe Bryant, Lebron James, Dwight Howard, Kevin Durant and other star players.
  • He wore the number 25 at Simeon High School in honor of the late Ben "Benji" Wilson, a Simeon prospect who was killed in 1984. During Derrick's tenure with the New York Knicks he also wore No. 25.
  • Was one of the highest ranked high school players in the country along with names like OJ Mayo, Kevin Love, Eric Gordon and Michael Beasley. He was scouted by schools such as Illinois, Kansas, Arizona, but signed with Memphis.
  • While at Memphis (2008), he led the Tigers to the Final Four, and most importantly, the championship game. He would go on to be drafted number 1 overall in the 2008 NBA draft by the Chicago Bulls.
  • In his first three years in the NBA he won the MVP (most valuable player) award, ROY (rookie of the year) award, 2-time All-Star, and All Rookie First Team. He also led the Chicago Bulls to the best record in the league and to the Eastern Conference Finals.
  • Unfortunately Derrick Rose's playstyle was a detriment to physical health (more specifically his knees), causing him to miss 27 games of the lockout season in 2011-2012. He dealt with injuries such as 'turf toe', sprained ankle, strained lower back, and an ACL-tear.
  • He's had multiple knee surgeries including three just with the Chicago Bulls alone.
  • Derrick Rose currently plays on the Detroit Pistons were he averages 18 PTS, 2.4 TRBS, 5.6 AST
  • One of Derrick's most memorable games in his professional career was on October 31st, 2018 when he scored 50 points with the Minnesota Timberwolves. During the game Derrick Rose was emotional because of all of the adversity he had to overcome (injuries, media, rape accusions).
